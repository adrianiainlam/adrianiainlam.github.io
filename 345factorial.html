\[30^x = 2^x\,3^x\,5^x\]

Let \(F(n)\) be the number of integers from 1 to 345 that has \(n\) as a factor.

Since \( 5 > 3 > 2\), it follows that \(F(5) < F(3) < F(2)\) (in other words, 5 occurs less frequently than 2 or 3). Hence, instead of 30 we only need to consider its factor 5.

\[\dfrac{345}{5} = 69 \qquad \therefore F(5) = 69\] \[\dfrac{345}{5^2} = 13.8 \qquad \therefore F(5^2) = 13\]

Similarly, \(F(5^3) = 2\), \(F(5^k) = 0\) for \(k > 3\). \[\therefore x = 69 + 13 + 2 = 84\]
